# Immunoengineering Lecture 2026
Slides and Notes – Dr. Gabriele Nasello  
Date: 20 March 2026

This repository contains the slide deck and detailed lecture notes for the session **“Immunoengineering and Biomaterials for Tissue Regeneration”**, delivered on 20 March 2026.

The materials provide an integrated overview of how the immune system shapes tissue repair, why immunomodulation is central to regenerative medicine, and how biomaterials and engineering strategies can guide immune responses toward constructive healing.

## 🧠 Lecture Summary

The lecture covers three central themes:

### 1. **Fundamentals of the immune system**
- Innate and adaptive immunity  
- Major immune cell populations  
- Sterile inflammation after injury  
- Introduction to flow cytometry for immunophenotyping

### 2. **The immune system as a regulator of regeneration**
- How early inflammatory events shape healing trajectories  
- Macrophage polarization and plasticity  
- T cell subsets as determinants of repair or fibrosis  
- Importance of effector/regulatory T cell balance  
- Peripheral T cell signatures as biomarkers for healing

### 3. **Engineering strategies to modulate immunity**
- Biomaterial physical cues (stiffness, shape, topography)  
- Anti inflammatory and pro resolving molecules  
- Sequential and localized delivery strategies  
- ECM based and non fouling interface designs  
- Gene and cell free biologics  
- Case studies in bone regeneration and transplant tolerance  
  (e.g., Treg induction hydrogels and FasL microgels)

---

## 🎓 Learning Objectives

By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:

- Describe how innate and adaptive immune responses contribute to tissue repair.  
- Explain why uncontrolled inflammation leads to regeneration failure.  
- Identify engineering principles used to design immune instructive biomaterials.  
- Interpret how macrophage and T cell subsets influence regenerative outcomes.  
- Understand emerging immunoengineering approaches for organ and cell transplantation.
